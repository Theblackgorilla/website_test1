Classical Hollywood

Time Period

1927-1969

Characteristics

  • A main struggle to solve a goal
  • A clear victory or defeat at the end of the movie
  • A conflict with other people or external circumstances

End of the Golden Age

By the 1960s, the golden age of Hollywood was at an end due to:

  • Retirement or death of actors from this period
  • Competition from TV, which was becoming popular
  • Movies coming from foreign countries

The Big Five Studios

  • 20th Century Fox
  • Warner Brothers
  • RKO
  • Metro Goldwyn Mayer (MGM)
  • Paramount Pictures

These five studios owned the majority of all production, distribution, and marketing companies. They were all vertically integrated.

1948 Supreme Court Ruling

In 1948, the Supreme Court ruled against the Big Five, stating that Hollywood had become oligarchical. This ended their monopoly over the film industry and opened opportunities for smaller directors.

The Little Three

  • Universal Pictures
  • United Artists
  • Columbia Pictures

Studio Control

The Big Five studios had tight control over their directors and production crew, meaning the studio heads had the last say over all aspects of the filmmaking process.

Alfred Hitchcock

Early Career

Alfred Hitchcock (1899-1980) was a British-American film director who started his career in the British film industry before moving to Hollywood.

Famous Works

Some of his famous movies include:

  • Psycho
  • Vertigo
  • Rear Window
  • North by Northwest

Style and Themes

His films frequently contain themes of false charges, complex characters, and twist endings, producing a distinct cinematic style that has influenced other filmmakers.

Awards

Despite receiving five Academy Award nominations for Best Director, he never won. However, he was honored with the Irving G. Thalberg Memorial Award in 1968.
