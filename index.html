Classical Hollywood Era (1927-1969)

Characteristics

  • A main struggle to solve a goal
  • A clear victory or defeat at the end of the movie
  • A conflict with other people or external circumstances

End of the Golden Age

By the 1960s, the Golden Age of Hollywood was coming to an end due to:

  • Retirement or death of actors from this period
  • Competition from TV, which was becoming popular
  • Influx of movies from foreign countries

The Big Five Studios

These studios owned the majority of all production, distribution, and marketing companies:

  • 20th Century Fox
  • Warner Brothers
  • RKO
  • Metro Goldwyn Mayer (MGM)
  • Paramount Pictures

Fun Fact: In 1948, the Supreme Court ruled against the Big Five, stating that Hollywood had become oligarchical.

The Little Three

  • Universal Pictures
  • United Artists
  • Columbia Pictures

Spotlight: Alfred Hitchcock (1899-1980)

British-American film director known for:

  • Groundbreaking psychological thrillers
  • Detailed storytelling
  • Famous works: Psycho, Vertigo, Rear Window, North by Northwest
  • Themes: false charges, complex characters, twist endings
  • Accolades: Five Academy Award nominations for Best Director, Irving G. Thalberg Memorial Award (1968)
